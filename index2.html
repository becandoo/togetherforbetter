Everyone has a story...

When I was 12, going on 13, I remember asking (ok begging) my parents for a computer. I must admit, I pulled the whole "I will do anything you ask, and be good all the time", etc. etc. but I was consistantly met with "No". Eventually, I stopped the nagging and figured I could always try again next year. When the day finally arrived, I was excited for cake and presents regardless. It was a school day, so everything was normal until I arrived home and went to drop my pack off in my room. I walked in, through my bag on my bed and just as I turned to walk back into the hall, I realized there was a giant present sitting on top of my desk. I turned and quickly walked back towards the living room, and in a very authoritive manner, asked "Why is there a giant present on my desk?"

My mom looked up and smiled "Most kids would be more grateful and just say thank you. They don't always get what they ask for on their birthday." As she spoke those last words, my stomach started twisting in an excited manner and I ran back down the hall, into my bedroom, silently hoping it was not a cruel joke, and began tearing the box open.

I could not believe it, as I lived and breathed, my very own computer...

Thus began my journey into the world of technology. I began taking as many computer related courses as possible in school, my favorite one was called "Technological Applications and required us to work with hardware and software related technologies.

As time went on and my educational journey required me to narrow in on a more focused area of technology, I chose to work with code, persuing a degreee in programming.
